# Review your audit history

Your personal audit history shows security-relevant activity for your
account. Use it to confirm recent sign-ins, password changes, and
two-factor authentication changes.

## Before you start

- You must be signed in.
- Audit entries are read-only. You cannot edit or delete them.

## Steps

1. Open **Account Settings**.
2. Select the **My Activity** tab.
3. Review the newest entries first.
4. Use the **Action** filter to focus on a specific event type.
5. Use pagination controls when more entries are available.

## What you can see

| Event | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| `LOGIN_SUCCESS` | Your account signed in successfully. |
| `LOGIN_FAILED` | A sign-in attempt failed. |
| `LOGOUT` | A browser session signed out. |
| `PASSWORD_CHANGE` | Your password changed. |
| `2FA_ENABLED` | Two-factor authentication was enabled. |
| `2FA_DISABLED` | Two-factor authentication was disabled. |

## What to check next

- If you do not recognize a successful sign-in, change your password
  and select **Sign out from all other devices**.
- If you see repeated failed sign-ins, make sure your password is unique
  and 2FA is enabled.
- If you do not recognize a 2FA change, contact support through your
  normal support channel.
